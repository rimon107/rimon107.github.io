Greetings!

Greetings!

I am Md Nahidul Islam, a Ph.D. student in Computer Science at the University of Memphis, advised by Dr. Myounggyu Won. My research focuses on trustworthy autonomous driving — specifically, interactive motion planning, trajectory forecasting, conformal risk certification, counterfactual evaluation, and model compression for safe robotics. I work at the intersection of machine learning and safety-critical decision-making, building methods that produce reliability guarantees for the systems as they are actually deployed, not just for predictors in isolation.

In May 2026 I earned my M.S. en route, with my thesis “BucketKD: A Safety-Aware Bucket-Based Knowledge Distillation Framework for End-to-End Motion Planning,” which compresses autonomous-driving planners while preserving safety-relevant trajectory behavior. I currently have manuscripts under review at NeurIPS 2026 and IROS 2026, and a paper recently accepted at IEEE MILCOM 2025.

Before joining the University of Memphis, I worked as an Assistant Director at Bangladesh Bank, the Central Bank of Bangladesh, and as a Software Engineer at Binate Solutions Ltd. I was previously a Research Assistant at Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology (BUET) under Dr. Muhammad Abdullah Adnan, where I contributed to publications at ICASSP, NAACL, and LREC.

I am actively seeking Summer 2027 research internships in autonomous driving, motion planning, trajectory forecasting, and trustworthy machine learning. If you are recruiting in these areas, please reach out by email.
