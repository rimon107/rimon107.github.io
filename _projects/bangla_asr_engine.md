**Bangla Automated Speech Recognition Engine** was a Samsung Research Bangladesh-funded research project on low-resource Bangla speech recognition, voice command understanding, and speech/text resource development.

The project explored end-to-end automatic speech recognition (ASR) for Bangla, including speech corpus preparation, grapheme-to-phoneme modeling, and contextual rescoring for voice command recognition. This work contributed to peer-reviewed publications at **ICASSP 2020**, **LREC 2020**, and **NAACL 2019**.

### Highlights

- Worked on a Samsung Research Bangladesh-funded Bangla ASR research project.
- Contributed to an end-to-end Bangla voice command recognition system using contextual rescoring.
- Explored topic-modeling-based contextual information to improve voice command recognition.
- Helped prepare Bangla speech corpus resources from publicly available audio and text.
- Worked on grapheme-to-phoneme modeling for non-trivial Bangla transcription problems.
- Built and evaluated sequence-to-sequence and attention-based models for Bangla ASR and text/speech processing.
- Contributed to research publications at ICASSP, LREC, and NAACL.
